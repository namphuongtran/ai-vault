## What it is

**Responsible AI** is about building and using AI in ways that are fair, transparent, and
accountable — especially for sensitive domains.

## Sensitive domains

For high-stakes problems such as **hiring, credit, insurance, healthcare, or education**, a
good solution usually considers:

- Fairness
- Bias testing
- Explainability
- Transparency
- Privacy
- Human oversight
- Auditability and traceability
- Risk management
- Clear limits on where the model may be used

> AI should not make final decisions in high-impact situations without appropriate human
> oversight. For example, AI can help screen job applications, but a human should review the
> final decision — and the team should check whether the training data disadvantages any
> group.

## Concepts to distinguish

- **Hallucination** — plausible-sounding output that is inaccurate or unsupported.
- **Toxicity** — offensive, hateful, harmful, or inappropriate content.
- **Plagiarism** — content copied or too close to a source without proper attribution.
- **Privacy** — risk of exposing or misusing personal and sensitive data.
- **Stereotyping** — generating or reinforcing prejudices about a group of people.
- **Interpretability** — how well a human can understand how the model works.
- **Explainability** — the ability to explain why a specific prediction was made.
- **Transparency** — how openly the system's build, evaluation, and use are disclosed.
